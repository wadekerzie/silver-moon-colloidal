How It Works

Our generators use a controlled electrolysis process to produce colloidal silver nanoparticles from 99.99% pure silver electrodes in distilled or reverse-osmosis water.

01

Electrolysis

A precisely regulated constant current passes through 99.99% pure silver electrodes submerged in distilled or reverse-osmosis water, releasing silver nanoparticles into suspension.

02

Constant Current Drive

Our proprietary regulation circuit maintains a perfectly steady current throughout the generation cycle, ensuring consistent nanoparticle size for uniform, high-quality colloidal silver.

03

Alternating Polarity

Electrode polarity alternates automatically during generation, minimizing silver oxide buildup on the electrodes and extending their operational life significantly.

04

Intelligent Auto-Shutoff

The generator continuously monitors the PPM concentration during electrolysis. When your desired target is reached (selectable from 5 to 25 PPM), generation stops automatically. This ensures consistent ionic silver production — the foundation for the reduction process that follows.

05

The Reduction Process

After electrolysis produces ionic silver, the critical reduction step converts silver ions into colloidal nanoparticles. This is where true colloidal silver is created — and where 70% of commercial manufacturers stop short. Silver Moon provides complete protocols covering this essential process.

Quality Verification

The Simple Color Test

True colloidal silver should be yellow or golden — not clear

Ionic Silver Only

Clear & colorless. Only ionic silver present — the reduction step was skipped.

True Colloidal Silver

Yellow or golden color from nanoparticle plasmon absorption. This is the real thing.

Poor Quality

Cloudy or gray. Particles are too large and will settle out of suspension over time.

If your colloidal silver is clear, you’ve only made ionic silver (no nanoparticles). True colloidal silver nanoparticles create a yellow or golden color due to plasmon absorption — a well-documented optical phenomenon. This simple visual test tells you if you’ve made the real thing.
