Why Most Commercial Products Fail — And How To Do It Right

A 2020 study published in the International Journal of Nanomedicine revealed that 70% of commercial colloidal silver products contain only ionic silver, not true colloidal nanoparticles. Here’s the critical insight: all generators produce ionic silver during electrolysis — that’s basic electrochemistry. The difference between ionic silver and true colloidal silver isn’t the equipment used — it’s whether the manufacturer completes the reduction process that converts ionic silver into nanoparticles. Most commercial producers skip this step, either through ignorance or cost-cutting. Silver Moon provides both professional-grade equipment AND the complete production knowledge.

01

Understanding the Two-Stage Process

  • Stage 1 — Electrolysis: Produces ionic silver (Ag+) in water — this happens with ALL generators
  • Stage 2 — Reduction: Converts ionic silver to colloidal silver nanoparticles — this is the critical step most manufacturers skip
  • The yellow color indicates successful reduction to nanoparticles
  • Colorless solutions contain only ionic silver (reduction step was skipped or incomplete)
02

Why 70% of Commercial Products Fail

  • They use generators (sometimes even good ones) to produce ionic silver
  • They skip the reduction step that creates true colloidal nanoparticles
  • They bottle and sell ionic silver labeled as “colloidal silver”
  • Result: Colorless ionic silver solutions misrepresented as colloidal silver

Production Setup & Water Quality

  • Use only distilled or reverse-osmosis (RO) water
  • Ensure water is free from minerals and contaminants
  • Clean glass containers only (never plastic or metal)
  • Proper electrode spacing and positioning

06

The Reduction Stage

This is the critical step that creates true colloidal nanoparticles — and where 70% of commercial manufacturers stop short.

Detailed reduction process instructions provided with equipment purchase.

Successful reduction converts ionic silver to nanoparticles, producing the characteristic yellow color from plasmon absorption.

07

Quality Verification

  • Color check: True colloidal silver should be yellow (pale yellow at 10 PPM, deeper yellow at higher concentrations)
  • If solution remains colorless, reduction was incomplete or skipped
  • Seek third-party lab testing for commercial production
  • UV-Vis spectroscopy shows plasmon absorption band (scientific verification)
08

Troubleshooting

  • Solution remains colorless — Reduction step incomplete or skipped
  • Cloudy or gray solution — Indicates contamination or excessive particle size
  • Inconsistent results — Verify process parameters at both stages
  • Black residue on electrodes — Normal silver oxide buildup; clean between batches
09

Storage & Shelf Life

  • Store in amber or cobalt blue glass bottles (protects from light degradation)
  • Keep away from electromagnetic fields
  • Typical shelf life: 6–12 months when stored properly
  • Color fading indicates particle aggregation
