01

Understanding the Two-Stage Process

  • Stage 1 — Electrolysis: Produces ionic silver (Ag+) in water — this happens with ALL generators
  • Stage 2 — Reduction: Converts ionic silver to colloidal silver nanoparticles — this is the critical step most manufacturers skip
  • The yellow color indicates successful reduction to nanoparticles
  • Colorless solutions contain only ionic silver (reduction step was skipped or incomplete)

The Reduction Stage

Detailed reduction process instructions provided with equipment purchase.

Successful reduction converts ionic silver to nanoparticles, producing the characteristic yellow color from plasmon absorption.

07

Quality Verification

  • Color check: True colloidal silver should be yellow (pale yellow at 10 PPM, deeper yellow at higher concentrations)
  • If solution remains colorless, reduction was incomplete or skipped
  • Seek third-party lab testing for commercial production
  • UV-Vis spectroscopy shows plasmon absorption band (scientific verification)
08

Troubleshooting

  • Solution remains colorless — Reduction step incomplete or skipped
  • Cloudy or gray solution — Indicates contamination or excessive particle size
  • Inconsistent results — Verify process parameters at both stages
  • Black residue on electrodes — Normal silver oxide buildup; clean between batches
09

Storage & Shelf Life

  • Store in amber or cobalt blue glass bottles (protects from light degradation)
  • Keep away from electromagnetic fields
  • Typical shelf life: 6–12 months when stored properly
  • Color fading indicates particle aggregation
