How It Works

Our generators use a controlled electrolysis process to produce colloidal silver nanoparticles from 99.99% pure silver electrodes in distilled or reverse-osmosis water.

01

Electrolysis

A precisely regulated constant current passes through 99.99% pure silver electrodes submerged in distilled or reverse-osmosis water, releasing silver nanoparticles into suspension.

02

Constant Current Drive

03

Alternating Polarity

Electrode polarity alternates automatically during generation, minimizing silver oxide buildup on the electrodes and extending their operational life significantly.

Certified Production Protocol

How to Produce Authentic, Scientifically Verified Colloidal Silver

In a market where 70% of commercial products contain only ionic silver (not true colloidal silver nanoparticles), knowing how to produce and verify authentic colloidal silver is critical. This protocol ensures you achieve professional-grade results every time.

01

Understanding True Colloidal Silver

True colloidal silver contains suspended silver nanoparticles, not dissolved silver ions. The critical difference is visible:

True colloidal silver should be yellow in color due to plasmon absorption. If your solution is colorless, it likely contains only ionic silver.

A 2020 study published in the International Journal of Nanomedicine analyzed 14 top-selling colloidal silver products and found that 70% contained only ionic silver, despite being labeled as “colloidal silver.”

02

Production Setup & Water Quality

  • Use only distilled or reverse-osmosis (RO) water
  • Ensure water is free from minerals and contaminants
  • Clean glass containers only (never plastic or metal)
  • Proper electrode spacing and positioning
03

Production Process

  • Follow step-by-step instructions with timing guidelines
  • Target PPM concentrations in the 5–25 PPM range
  • Monitor visual indicators: color develops from clear to yellow
  • Auto-shutoff ensures precision — set your target and walk away
04

Quality Verification

  • Color check: Pale yellow at 10 PPM, deeper yellow at higher concentrations
  • Colorless solution likely contains ionic silver only
  • Seek third-party lab testing for commercial production
  • Simple at-home tests to verify nanoparticle presence
05

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Solution remains colorless — Check water quality, electrode condition, or current settings
  • Cloudy or gray solution — Indicates contamination or excessive particle size
  • Inconsistent results — Verify constant current and proper electrode maintenance
  • Black residue on electrodes — Normal silver oxide buildup; clean with fine sandpaper between batches
06

Storage & Shelf Life

  • Store in amber or cobalt blue glass bottles (protects from light degradation)
  • Keep away from electromagnetic fields
  • Typical shelf life: 6–12 months when stored properly
  • Color fading indicates particle aggregation
